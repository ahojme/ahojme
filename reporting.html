Data visualisation for digital reporting

Developing the guidelines • 2019

My roles

Design and project leader- from concept to execution.

Goal

Create a common look for data visualisation for internal and external reporting systems in the organisation.

Challenges

  • The main brand colors in graphs did not provide good contrast.
  • Defining guidelines regarding colour palette, text and shape, which would fit in all applications (various chart types and interfaces).

Approach

  • Interviews with owners of reporting tools to discover possibilities and constraints, and get a feel of audience needs.
  • Research best practices for data visualisation and accessibility.
  • I prioritised designing for the most common charts.
  • Iterative work on the solution, tested with users.

Results

  • Data visualisation concept and examples of correct graphs and charts.
  • Guidelines and best practices for data visualisation including guidance for colours, fonts, shapes of graphs, layout of dashboards, iconography, best practices, accessibility.

Colours

The colours used in the visualizations must be on-brand and fulfil several roles: they distinguish categories, emphasise data, carry messages.

Primary colour scheme

This colour scheme is dedicated for use in reporting tools. It is based on the brand colours - dark blue, grey and their shades. Therefore, the biggest challenge was to make the adjacent colours distinctive from each other.

Representing quantity

The gradations of the main brand colour are used to represent data values in heat maps. Colour intensity emphasises the strength of a given variable.

Highlights

The primary brand colour is used to highlight e.g. extraordinary performance.

Shapes

Bar charts have sharp corners, so the length of the bar can be precisely measured.

Pie charts resemble doughnuts.

Typography

Consistent typographic style asserts the structure of the report and helps to emphasise the key data. The preferred font is Segoe UI and fallback fonts are also specified.

Dashboard layout

This sample layout shows good data visualization practices. The complementary guidelines prescribe rules for prioritisation, focusing the audience's attention and communicating a story with a dashboard.
